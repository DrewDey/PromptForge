# Platform Concept Document

## The Idea
A community hub where anyone — from AI beginners to power users — can discover, share, and build with AI prompts and workflows. Think of it as **GitHub meets Pinterest for AI prompts**, organized by real-world use cases.

## Why This Matters
1. **The gap is real** — AI tools are everywhere, but most people don't know what to do with them
2. **Prompts are the new skill** — knowing how to talk to AI is becoming as important as knowing how to Google
3. **No good central hub exists** — prompts are scattered across Twitter threads, Reddit posts, Discord servers, and YouTube videos
4. **The chain is the magic** — single prompts are useful, but the real power is in multi-step workflows that most people never discover

## User Journey Examples

### Beginner (Sarah, small business owner)
1. Lands on the platform, browses "Finance" category
2. Finds "Generate a monthly cash flow forecast" — a single prompt
3. Copies it, pastes into ChatGPT, gets immediate value
4. Bookmarks it, starts exploring more

### Intermediate (Marcus, marketing manager)
1. Searches for "content calendar"
2. Finds a 3-step prompt chain: research → outline → full calendar
3. Follows the chain, gets a complete content calendar
4. Tweaks it for his industry, shares his version back

### Advanced (Priya, developer)
1. Browses "API Workflows" section
2. Finds a multi-step chain that connects Claude API → spreadsheet → email automation
3. Forks it, modifies the API calls for her use case
4. Publishes her version with documentation

## What a "Prompt" Looks Like on the Platform
- **Title** — clear, descriptive name
- **Description** — what it does and who it's for
- **Category** — primary domain (finance, marketing, etc.)
- **Tags** — searchable keywords
- **Difficulty level** — beginner / intermediate / advanced
- **The prompt(s)** — the actual text, with placeholders highlighted
- **Model recommendation** — which AI model works best
- **Example output** — what you should expect to get
- **Chain steps** (if multi-step) — ordered sequence with explanations
- **Author** — who created/shared it

## What Makes This Different
| Other platforms | This platform |
|---|---|
| Focus on one model | Model-agnostic |
| Just raw prompts | Full context: description, examples, chains |
| No organization | Organized by domain and difficulty |
| Tech-focused audience | Everyone, especially non-technical |
| Static content | Community-driven, living library |
